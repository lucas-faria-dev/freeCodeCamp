Find out more about the Three Brazilian Heroes: On April 14, 1945, during a patrol in the vicinity of Montese, three soldiers: Geraldo Baêta da Cruz, then 28 years old, a native of Entre Rios de Minas; Arlindo Lúcio da Silva, 25, from São João del-Rei; and Geraldo Rodrigues de Souza, 26, from Rio Preto, The three heroes served in the 11th Expeditionary Infantry Regiment and died as heroes in Montese, Italy, the scene of one of the bloodiest battles of the conflict with the participation of the FEB As part of a patrol, the three miners came face to face with an entire German company. They were ordered to surrender, but continued in combat "until the last cartridge," as they say in the barracks. Machine-gunned on April 14, 1945, they received, instead of the mass grave, the special honors of the German army. He was the son of Maria Cipriana de Jesus and João Olímpio da Silva. A poor young man raised without the care of his father, because he was mentally ill, Arlindo was admitted to the "Padre Sacramento School for the Preservation of Minors". She finished primary school in 1934, leaving school at the request of her mother at the age of 15. Then he went to work as a bricklayer's servant at Companhia Construtora Luiz Bacarini. As a good citizen and in the unrestrained desire to serve the Fatherland, and with his mother's consent, he voluntarily enlisted in the ranks of the Army. He was a true soldier. Experience the Battle of Monte Castello GERALDO RODRIGUES DE SOUZA Little is known about him, what we do know is that he was born in Rio Preto (Sta. Bárbara do Monte Verde / MG), in 1919. He belonged to the Command Company of the III Battalion of the 11th RIE and was the son of Josino Rodrigues de Souza and Maria Joana de Jesus. GERALDO BAÊTA DA CRUZ Também pouco se sabe dele , nasceu em 21 Jul 1916, na pequena cidade mineira de João Ribeiro. Era filho de Antônio José da Cruz e de Maria da Conceição da Cruz. Geraldo Baêta era enfermeiro cirúrgico, pertencente ao Destacamento de Saúde. Tinha a nobre missão de amparar aqueles que tombavam na área de sacrifício, prestando-lhes o apoio médico imediato. Meigs” rumo à distante Itália, onde já se encontrava o 1.º Escalão da Força Expedicionária Brasileira. Chegaram ao Porto de Nápoles, às 07:30 horas do dia 6 Out 1944, de onde partiram, no dia 9, para o Porto de Livorno. Três dias depois estavam desembarcando e seguindo para a chamada “Staging Area”, localizado a oeste da cidade de Pisa ( Vila Rosare ). No dia 1.º de dezembro de 1944, o 11.º RIE entrava em linha, sendo considerado em combate. Mas lá estavam, os três Heróis do Brasil, três bravos soldados, de vida sofrida e que estavam dispostos a fazer de tudo para sua Pátria. Em 14 de abril de 1944 , foi o dia do ataque a Montese, na qual o combate seria, o mais sangrento que a FEB participaria Conheçam: Batalha de Collecchio – Os Pracinhas em ação Durante o ataque a Montese, o pelotão, ao qual faziam parte ARLINDO, GERALDO e BAÊTA, foi detido por violenta barragem de morteiros inimigos, enquanto uma metralhadora alemã hostilizava violentamente o seu flanco esquerdo, obrigando os atacantes a se manterem colados ao solo. Não tendo mais possibilidades de sair do local, nossos três heróis ficaram desgarrados de sua fração. Mas não temeram! Imediatamente, o Soldado ARLINDO, atirador de fuzil automático, localiza a resistência inimiga e num gesto de grande bravura, gesto esse de um menino acostumado a enfrentar as dificuldades da vida que levara até então, levanta-se, localiza a resistência inimiga e sobre ela despeja seis carregadores da sua arma. Geraldo and Baêta don't stop shooting. There were three small squares against a large troop. This troop thought it was facing another troop equal to or greater than its own, because the intrepid Brazilians did not stop shooting. They shot until they ran out of ammunition. ARLINDO is mortally wounded by an enemy sniper, while GERALDO is hit by a cruel piece of shrapnel and BAÊTA receives a well-aimed shot... The tombs of the three Brazilian heroes The tombs of the three heroes. Admiring the courage and resistance of the miners, the commander had them buried in a shallow grave and placed a cross and a plaque with the inscription: Drei brasilianische helden, which in good Portuguese means "three Brazilian heroes". After the war, they were transferred to the cemetery of Pistoia, in Italy, and then to the Monument to the Pracinhas
